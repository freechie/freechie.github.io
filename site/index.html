About

Based in Northern NJ. Before finding my love for programming, I loved and love the arts. At 13, my friends convinced me to learn guitar for a metal band they were forming. They wanted me to be their rhythm guitarist, so I learned from my friends, on my own, and through the internet.

A few grassroots records and bands later, I stumbled upon interactive digital art installations while visiting art museums in NYC with a close friend.

I knew then I wanted to dive into the world of interactive digital art.

I thought to first learn to do that, I had to get really good at writing code and math, so I decided to switch from Media Arts to Computer Science.

Since graduation, I continue to learn foundational math, statistics, and computer science while learning the Software Development Lifecycle akin to problem solving, and performant, scalable enterprise systems of all kinds while dipping into p5js sketches for fun.
